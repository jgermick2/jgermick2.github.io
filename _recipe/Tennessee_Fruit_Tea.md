## Background

Living in Murfreesboro, I learned that no good Tennessee brunch would be complete without some version of this fruit tea. Add lemon wedges, pineapple slices, and/or mint leaves for garnish

## Ingredients

- 18 to 24 tea bags (depending on how strong you want the tea flavor)
- 1 1/2 quarts boiling water 
- 2 cups granulated sugar
- 48 ounces canned pineapple juice
- 12 ounce canned frozen orange juice concentrate
- 12 ounces canned frozen lemonade concentrate

## Directions

1. Place 18 to 24 tea bags in bowl or pitcher. Pour 1 1/2 quarts of boiling water over tea bags. Add the 2 cups of sugar, stirring to dissolve.
2. Let tea bags steep in the sugar water for several hours or overnight. 
3. Squeeze tea bags to wring out the extra flavor before discarding.
4. Pour the tea into a large bowl or pan. Add the cans of pineapple juice, frozen orange juice, and lemonade.
5. Stir until the sugar has dissolved and the concentrate has thawed. Pour equal amounts into two one-gallon jugs.
6. Add cold water to each jug to fill and refrigerate until thoroughly chilled. Shake well before serving.
