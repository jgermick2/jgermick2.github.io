## Background

Time consuming but way worth it! This one has become a "must have" at our Thanksgiving dinner.

## Ingredients

- 2 tbsp sugar 
- 1 1/2 tsp salt
- 2 pkg active dry yeast 
- 3 c. flour
- 1 1/4 c. milk 
- 1 c. butter
- 1 egg
- 1 tbsp water

## Directions

1. Combine sugar, salt, yeast and 1 c. flour. Heat milk until very warm (120- 130). With mixture at low speed, beat liquid into dry ingredients until just blended. 
2. At medium speed, beat 2 min. Beat in 1/2 c. flour to make thick batter. 
3. Beat 2 min more. Stir in about 1 c. flour. On floured surface, kneed dough until smooth and elastic. 
4. Shape into ball. place in large greased bowl, turning to grease top. Cover, let rise until doubled (1 1/2 hours). 
5. Punch down dough. Turn onto lightly floured surface. Cover with bowl. Let rest 30 min. 
6. Roll butter between 2 sheets of waxed paper to 10x7 rectangle. Roll dough to 18x9. 
7. Remove 1 sheet of paper from butter and invert onto dough. Remove other sheet. Fold unbuttered dough over butter. Fold buttered dough over center to make 9x6 rectangle. Press edges to seal. Carefully roll to 18x9 rectangle. Fold in thirds to make 9x6 rectangle. Sprinkle with flour, wrap, chill for 2 hours. 
8. Roll, fold and chill twice more (may leave dough overnight after final roll). Cut dough in half. Roll each piece into 21x10 rectangle. Cut triangles 7 inches wide at base. 
9. Roll triangles and place 2 in apart on cookie sheet. Bend to crescent shape. 
10. Cover with plastic wrap and let rise until doubled (1hr).
11. Preheat oven to 425. Brush with beaten egg and water. Bake 15 min or until puffed and browned. Cool on rack 10 min.
