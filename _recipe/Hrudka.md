## Background

Aunt Mary Holman would make this for us at Easter time.

She taught Judi, Julie and I how to make it. You are supposed to cook this over a very low heat and stir it constantly. It takes forever. Aunt Mary smaked Judi with a wooden spoon because she said Judi wasn't stirring fast enough. We probably turned up the heat so it would be done faster. Aunt Mary noticed. She flew across the kitchen to turn the heat down. Julie was in her way, so she shoved Julie out of the way. It was intense. Aunt Mary said that was nothing. She claimed, we should have seen how she was treated when her Mom showed her how to make Hrudka.

## Ingredients

-12 eggs
-1 quart whole milk 
-1/2 tsp salt
-1/4 tsp pepper Cheesecloth.

Some recipes add 1/2 tsp cinnamon, 1 tbsp of vanilla, a cup of sugar and/or nutmeg to taste.

## Directions

1. Crack eggs into a large saucepan. 
2. Add milk, salt and pepper. 
3. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly. 
4. Cook until the mixture forms curds and the whey separates. This will take at least 30 minutes.

5. Drain the mixture into a colander lined with cheesecloth.
6. Tightly wrap the cheese cloth around the drained mixture and tie the cheesecloth with a twist tie. 
7. Hang for at least 2 hours or overnight. 
8. Remove cheesecloth. Wrap Hrudka in damp linen, place in a bowl and keep refrigerated.
