## Background

This is Grandma Joan Carmody's recipe.

Use old bread, buns or rolls. I always double the recipe. Mom used more bread than the recipe calls for because we liked the pudding thicker. If you double the recipe, you have to increase the time. Try adding a 1/2 hour when doubling the recipe. The pudding should be slightly browned when done. It looks beautiful when you take it out of the oven, but it will fall.

Mom added the golden raisins because of Dad. The original recipe calls for natural raisins.

I also approximated the amount of raisins to use.

## Ingredients

- 3 slices bread
- 2 cups milk
- 2 eggs
- 1/2 cup sugar 
- 1/2 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp cinnamon 
- 1/4 tsp mace
- 1 tsp vanilla 
- 1/2 cup raisins
- 1/2 cup golden raisins

## Directions

1. Soak bread in 2 cups of milk for 10 minutes. Add eggs, sugar, salt cinnamon & mace. Mix with an egg mixer. Add vanilla & mix. Add raisins, mix slightly to blend raisins evenly
2. Place in pan of water & bake for 1 hour at 325 degree oven.
